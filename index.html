Effectiveness Of
Flexible Working Practices

üîç Context:
Analysed UK employer data from the Work-Life Balance Survey (WLB4) to examine how Flexible Working Practices (FWPs) impact organisational productivity, using employee behaviour (motivation, absenteeism, turnover) as mediators.

üí° Tasks:
Extracted and cleaned data from 1,388 profit-driven firms. Performed exploratory analysis and built ordinal logistic regression, decision tree, and random forest models to predict productivity. Applied feature importance analysis and interpreted results using Contingency Theory.

üìà Key Outcomes:
Demonstrated that FWPs improve productivity, especially in medium-sized firms, through enhanced motivation and reduced turnover. Highlighted the importance of formal policies and role suitability.

R, RStudio, ggplot2, ordinal, randomForest, CART, Excel
